Gwalior

Gwalior Fort

Gwalior Fort, standing on a sandstone hill, is a formidable structure with a rich history. The fort complex includes Man Singh Palace, Teli Ka Mandir, and the Saas Bahu Temples, showcasing a blend of Rajput and Mughal architectural styles.

Jai Vilas Palace

Jai Vilas Palace, the opulent residence of the Scindia family, combines Italian, Corinthian, and Tuscan architectural styles. The palace, now a museum, houses the world's largest chandelier, grand halls, and an extensive collection of artifacts.

Sas Bahu Temples

The Sas Bahu Temples, dedicated to Lord Vishnu, are intricate structures within the Gwalior Fort complex. The temples, adorned with detailed carvings, reflect the artistic brilliance of the Kachchhapaghata dynasty.

Gujari Mahal Archaeological Museum

The Gujari Mahal Archaeological Museum, within the Gwalior Fort, houses rare artifacts and sculptures. The museum, originally built by Raja Man Singh for his consort Mrignayani, provides insights into Gwalior's cultural and historical heritage.

Tansen's Tomb

Tansen's Tomb, dedicated to the legendary musician Tansen, is a serene spot in Gwalior. The tomb's simple yet elegant architecture pays homage to one of the greatest musicians in Indian history.

Sun Temple

The Sun Temple, dedicated to the Sun God, is a modern architectural marvel in Gwalior. With intricate carvings and a tranquil setting, the temple attracts both spiritual seekers and admirers of artistic beauty.
