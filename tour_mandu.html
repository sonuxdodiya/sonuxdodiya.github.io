Mandu (Mandav)

Jahaz Mahal

Mandu, with its historic charm, features Jahaz Mahal, a magnificent palace resembling a ship. Overlooking the Munj Talab, this architectural marvel reflects the rich history and cultural grandeur of Mandu.

Roopmati's Pavilion

Roopmati's Pavilion, perched on a hill, offers panoramic views of the Narmada Valley. This romantic setting, associated with the love story of Baz Bahadur and Roopmati, adds a touch of mystique to Mandu's historical landscape.

Hindola Mahal

Hindola Mahal, or the Swinging Palace, showcases Mandu's medieval architecture. The inclined walls and large halls create an impressive structure that once served as an audience hall for the rulers of Malwa.

Rani Roopmati's Mosque

Rani Roopmati's Mosque, situated near Roopmati's Pavilion, is an elegant structure with domes and arches. The mosque, named after the queen, adds to the historical and architectural richness of Mandu.

Baz Bahadur's Palace

Baz Bahadur's Palace, nestled amid scenic surroundings, was the residence of Mandu's ruler. The palace, with its courtyards and water features, narrates tales of Mandu's royal history and artistic finesse.

Ashrafi Mahal

Ashrafi Mahal, an ancient madrasa and tomb, reflects the fusion of Islamic and Hindu architectural styles. The intricate carvings and historical significance make it a fascinating site for those exploring Mandu's cultural heritage.
