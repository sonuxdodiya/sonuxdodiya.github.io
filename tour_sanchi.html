Sanchi

Sanchi Stupa

Sanchi, an ancient Buddhist complex, is renowned for its Great Stupa. The Sanchi Stupa, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a masterpiece of ancient Indian architecture, adorned with intricate carvings and stupas, symbolizing the life of Lord Buddha.

Ashoka Pillar

The Ashoka Pillar, standing tall near the Southern Gateway of the Great Stupa, features a four-lion capital and inscriptions by Emperor Ashoka. It serves as a significant historical artifact, reflecting Ashoka's embrace of Buddhism.

Sanchi Museum

Explore the Sanchi Museum, which houses a remarkable collection of artifacts excavated from the site. The museum provides insight into the cultural and historical significance of Sanchi through sculptures, relics, and ancient manuscripts.

Great Bowl

The Great Bowl, an enormous stone vessel carved out of a single piece of stone, is a notable structure at Sanchi. Believed to have been used for ritualistic purposes, it adds to the archaeological marvels of the site.

Gupta Temple

The Gupta Temple, dedicated to Lord Vishnu, is an ancient structure showcasing Gupta period architecture. The temple, adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures, adds to the spiritual and artistic ambiance of Sanchi.

Udayagiri Caves

Udayagiri Caves, located nearby, are rock-cut caves adorned with Jain sculptures and inscriptions. These caves provide a glimpse into the rich cultural and religious diversity that existed in the region during ancient times.
