Khajuraho

Khajuraho Temples

Khajuraho, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is renowned for its group of intricately carved temples. These temples, dedicated to various deities, feature exquisite sculptures and intricate details, showcasing the architectural brilliance of the Chandela dynasty.

Western Group of Temples

The Western Group of Temples in Khajuraho, including the Kandariya Mahadeva Temple and Lakshmana Temple, form the main attraction. Adorned with erotic sculptures and detailed carvings, these temples are a testament to Khajuraho's artistic and cultural heritage.

Eastern Group of Temples

The Eastern Group of Temples, including the Parsvanath Temple and Ghantai Temple, provides a glimpse into Jain architecture. These temples, with their serene ambiance and intricate sculptures, add to the spiritual and cultural diversity of Khajuraho.

Duladeo Temple

The Duladeo Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, stands as a fine example of Khajuraho's temple architecture. The temple's exquisite carvings and artistic details make it a captivating site for those interested in ancient Indian art.

Khajuraho Dance Festival

The Khajuraho Dance Festival, held against the backdrop of the temples, celebrates classical dance forms. Renowned artists showcase their talent, creating a cultural extravaganza that attracts enthusiasts from around the world.

Vishvanatha Temple

The Vishvanatha Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is a part of the Western Group. With its ornate architecture and detailed carvings, it contributes to Khajuraho's status as a UNESCO World Heritage site and a cultural treasure trove.
